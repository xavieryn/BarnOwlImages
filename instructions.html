Annotation Instructions: Fruit Pest Moths

Please annotate only if you are confident in the identification. Do not annotate if you are unsure. False positives can harm model accuracy.

1. Oriental Fruit Moth (OFM)

Look for a small moth with mottled brown wings and a slightly upturned tail. Often found on fruit surfaces (like peaches or apples).

2. Codling Moth (CM)

This moth has brownish-grey forewings with dark bands or spots near the tips. Wings have a "coppery" sheen when light hits at an angle. It often appears more compact than the OFM.

3. Obliquebanded Leafroller (OBLR)

Look for tan to reddish-brown wings with distinctive oblique (angled) dark bands across them. Wings are more textured or patterned than OFM or CM.

General Notes:

  • Only annotate the moths shown in the examples above.
  • Skip blurry or unclear images unless the match is very clear.
  • Do not annotate larvae, cocoons, or partially visible insects unless you are absolutely certain.
